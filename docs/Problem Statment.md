# Project Brief 


## Theme 3. Remote Housing Crisis centred in NT
As part of our project, we are developing an app to help tackle the current housing crisis in the NT. Currently, about 6000 people are on the public housing waitlist, and many homes face long delays for repairs and recycling for new tenants. We aim to help reduce the stresses and issues seen by many tenants when requesting repairs and thereby speeding up the processes to bring public houses back into the market and help maintain these assets viable for future tenants in the NT. This can help reduce the waiting lists for public housing by having more homes available sooner through current upkeep and quicker repairs.

# Purpose of the app

* Present our skills and technical knowledge
* Provide a useful and efficient app for tenants to request repairs
* Help to keep the quality and availability of NT public housing assets via prompt maintenance

# Target Users (Persona)

We aim as likely users of the app to be mainly both NT public housing clients and the various contractors and repairmen who will bid for work via the app. Other users will be staff at the NT Department of Housing, Local Government and Community. 

# Hypothesis

An accessible user friendly, visually appealing and efficient app will increase interactions and user engagement between tenants and tradesmen. Tradesmen will do the upkeep to ensure these assets the NT government owns remain viable and ready to use again with new tenants.

# Technical Implementation 

To ensure the app is scalable, maintainable and robust, the development will adhere to industry standard software engineering practices. This revolves around CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) life cycle for repair requests and asset management. 

# Conclusion

The web application is designed to provide a user-friendly and efficient platform for managing tenant–maintenance interactions. Additionally, the platform supports the ongoing upkeep of Northern Territory Government assets, ensuring they remain in suitable condition for future tenancy. By maintenance efficiency and asset readiness, particularly in remote areas, this initiative contributes to broader efforts to alleviate the housing crisis in the Northern Territory.
